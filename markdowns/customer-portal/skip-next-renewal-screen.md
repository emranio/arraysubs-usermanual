# User Guide
The **Skip Next Renewal** screen is part of the **Manage Your Subscription** area on the Subscription Details page.

It gives customers a middle-ground option between staying on the normal billing schedule and cancelling completely.

Customers usually reach it from:

- **My Account → Subscriptions → View subscription → Manage Your Subscription → Skip Next Renewal**

## What Skip Next Renewal means
Skipping a renewal lets the customer delay one or more billing cycles while keeping the subscription relationship intact.

This is useful when the customer:

- needs a short break
- has not fully used the current cycle yet
- wants more time before the next charge
- does not want to cancel permanently

## When the Skip section appears
The skip section only appears when:

- the store enabled skip renewal
- the store allows customers to use it
- the current subscription status is eligible, usually active or trial

If any of those conditions fail, customers will not see the tool.

## What the screen looks like when no skip is active
When the subscription is eligible and not currently skipping, customers see:

- a short explanation of what skipping does
- a **Skip Next Renewal** button

The surrounding text explains that the customer can skip a billing cycle without cancelling the subscription.

## Choosing how many cycles to skip
If the store allows more than one cycle to be skipped, the portal asks the customer how many billing cycles they want to skip.

For example, the customer may be prompted to enter a number between:

- 1 and the store’s maximum allowed cycles

If only one cycle is allowed, the choice is simpler and the customer moves straight toward confirmation.

## Confirmation step
Before the skip is applied, the portal asks for confirmation.

This prevents accidental changes and makes it clear that the renewal date is about to move.

## What customers see after a skip is active
When a subscription is already in skip mode, the screen changes from an action prompt into a status card.

Customers can see information such as:

- how many cycle(s) are currently being skipped
- when the skip was requested
- an **Undo Skip** button

This makes the current state obvious and helps customers reverse the change if they act too quickly.

## Undo Skip
If the store still allows the change to be undone, customers can click:

- **Undo Skip**

This restores the original renewal path rather than keeping the delayed cycle arrangement.

## Why merchants use this feature
Skip is useful for stores that want to reduce churn.

Customers who are not ready to continue right now often prefer skipping a cycle over:

- cancelling entirely
- contacting support for a one-off billing delay
- disputing a charge because they forgot about the renewal

## What customers should understand before using skip
Customers should know that skip is not the same as:

- a refund
- a pause with long-term suspension
- a permanent cancellation
- a plan downgrade

It is simply a structured delay for future billing cycles within the rules the store has configured.

# Use Case
A customer is about to travel for a month and will not use the service during that time. Instead of cancelling, they open the subscription detail page, click **Skip Next Renewal**, confirm the skip, and continue the subscription later when they are ready.

# FAQ
### Is skipping the same as cancelling?
No. Skipping delays renewal without ending the subscription.

### Can customers skip multiple cycles?
Sometimes. That depends on the maximum number of cycles the store allows.

### What happens after a skip is active?
The portal shows the current skip state and can offer an **Undo Skip** action.

### Will every subscription show Skip Next Renewal?
No. The feature must be enabled and the subscription must be in an eligible status.
