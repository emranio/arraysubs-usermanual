# User Guide
The **Vacation Mode** screen is the pause-and-resume tool inside the Customer Portal.

Customers usually find it in:

- **My Account → Subscriptions → View subscription → Manage Your Subscription → Vacation Mode**

This feature is designed for customers who want to temporarily stop the subscription without cancelling it permanently.

## What Vacation Mode does
Vacation Mode pauses the subscription for a period of time or until the customer resumes it.

This can help when the customer:

- will be away for a while
- does not need deliveries temporarily
- wants breathing room without ending the subscription relationship
- needs a longer break than a single skipped renewal cycle

## When the Vacation Mode section appears
Customers only see this section when:

- pause functionality is enabled
- customer pausing is allowed
- the subscription status is eligible for pausing or currently paused

That means the section may appear in two different states:

- the customer can pause now
- the customer is already paused and can resume

## What the section looks like before pausing
When the subscription is eligible and not currently paused, customers may see:

- a short explanation of Vacation Mode
- a **Pause Subscription** button

The supporting text explains that the subscription can be resumed later.

## Pause setup behavior
When the customer clicks **Pause Subscription**, the portal can ask for extra information depending on store rules.

### Duration
The portal can ask how many days the customer wants to pause.

That duration must stay within the store’s allowed maximum when a limit is configured.

### Reason
If the store requires a pause reason, the customer may also be asked to explain why they want to pause.

## Confirmation step
Before the pause is applied, the portal asks for confirmation.

This helps avoid accidental pauses and makes it clearer that the subscription will enter a non-active state.

## What the screen shows while paused
When a subscription is already paused, the Vacation Mode section becomes a status panel.

Customers may see:

- a clear **Subscription Paused** state
- the date when the pause started
- a scheduled resume date, if one exists
- a **Resume Now** button

This makes the pause state feel transparent rather than hidden.

## Resume Now
Customers can manually restore the subscription by clicking:

- **Resume Now**

This is useful when the customer returns earlier than expected and wants service to continue immediately.

## Automatic resumption
If the pause was created with a defined end date, the subscription can also resume automatically when that period ends.

From the customer point of view, that means they do not always need to come back manually if the pause was temporary and time-based.

## Vacation Mode versus Skip Next Renewal
Customers often confuse these two features.

### Skip Next Renewal
Best for a short billing delay or one-off cycle adjustment.

### Vacation Mode
Best for a more explicit temporary suspension period, especially when the customer expects the subscription to be paused for longer than a simple one-cycle skip.

# Use Case
A customer receives a recurring physical delivery but is out of town for six weeks. Instead of cancelling or repeatedly contacting support, they open the subscription detail page, use **Vacation Mode**, choose the pause duration, and later click **Resume Now** when they return.

# FAQ
### Is Vacation Mode permanent?
No. It is a temporary pause tool, not a cancellation.

### Can the customer resume before the scheduled end date?
Yes. If the portal shows **Resume Now**, the customer can resume early.

### Does the pause always need a reason?
No. That depends on the store’s settings.

### Is Vacation Mode the same as skipping a renewal?
No. Skip and pause are related but different tools with different customer intent.
