# User Guide
The **Store Credit** screen is a premium customer-account page that extends the Customer Portal experience in **Pro** stores.

Customers can usually find it here:

- **My Account → Store Credit**

This page is separate from the Subscriptions screen, but it lives in the same WooCommerce account area and is designed to feel like part of one unified account experience.

## What the Store Credit screen is for
The page helps customers answer questions such as:

- How much store credit do I have right now?
- Where did that credit come from?
- Has any credit expired or is it about to expire?
- Can I buy more credit for future purchases or renewals?

## Balance card at the top
The first thing the customer sees is the current balance card.

This card prominently displays:

- **Your Store Credit Balance**
- the formatted balance amount

If the store allows customers to buy more credit, the card can also show:

- **+ Buy More Credit**

## Purchase Store Credit area
When credit purchase is enabled, the page can include a **Purchase Store Credit** section.

### What this section does
It allows the customer to add more credit to their account for future use.

Depending on the store setup, customers may see one or both of these purchase styles:

- fixed-value credit products
- custom-amount credit products

### Fixed-value credit products
For fixed-value options, customers can usually see:

- the credit product name
- the credit amount
- the product price
- any bonus percentage
- a **Buy Now** button

### Custom-amount credit products
For flexible credit products, customers can usually:

- enter their own amount within the allowed min and max limits
- submit the form with **Add to Cart**

This is useful for stores that want to let customers top up exactly the amount they need.

## Bonus credit messaging
Some credit products may include a bonus incentive, such as extra credit for buying a certain amount.

When that applies, customers may see a visible bonus badge, for example:

- **+10% Bonus**

This makes top-up offers easier to understand without needing a separate promotional page.

## Expiring credit warning
If the customer has credit that will expire soon, the page can show a warning area.

### What the warning includes
The warning can tell the customer:

- how much credit is expiring
- that the expiry is within the next 30 days
- a direct link encouraging them to use it now

This is one of the most valuable parts of the screen because it turns store credit from “invisible money” into something the customer is more likely to use before it disappears.

## Transaction History table
The lower part of the screen shows the credit history.

### What customers can see
Each entry can show:

- date and time
- a title or description
- source label
- credited or debited amount
- expiry display when relevant

### Common source labels customers may encounter
Examples include:

- Refund
- Admin
- Subscription
- Order Payment
- Purchase
- Expiration
- Manual
- Adjustment

This makes the balance much easier to understand than a single number alone.

## Linked references
When a credit log relates to an order, the history can include a link to the related order number.

That is helpful for customers asking where a particular credit addition or use came from.

## Empty state
If the customer has no credit transactions yet, the screen does not show an empty table.

Instead, it shows a simple explanation that there are no credit transactions yet and reminds the customer that store credit can come from refunds, subscription rewards, or promotional offers.

## Pagination
The transaction history is paginated when the customer has many entries.

This keeps the page manageable and avoids an endlessly long account screen.

## Availability notes
The Store Credit screen only appears when the premium store credit feature is enabled.

If the feature is disabled, customers should not expect the menu item or the account page to remain available.

# Use Case
A customer receives store credit from a refund and later buys extra credit during a promotion. They open **My Account → Store Credit** to check the current balance, review where each credit entry came from, and confirm whether any portion is close to expiring.

# FAQ
### Is Store Credit part of the free portal?
No. It is a **Pro** extension.

### Can customers buy more credit from this page?
Yes, if the store enabled credit purchase options.

### Why does the page show positive and negative amounts?
Because it tracks both credit additions and credit usage.

### What if a customer has no credit history yet?
The page shows an empty-state message instead of a table.
